Welcome

A Journey of Growth

This isn't just a list of accomplishments. It's a story of discovering purpose through service, overcoming self-doubt, and learning that true leadership begins with lifting others. Scroll down to walk through the chapters of my life.

Chapter I

The Foundation: Learning to Serve

My story doesn't begin with grand ambitions, but as a quiet, timid kid in the Lower Nursery at Lyceum International School Wattala. Finding my voice and purpose took time, and it was through the discipline of Scouting that I truly began to understand the value of community and steady effort.

I wasn't chasing titles; I was focused on showing up and contributing. That daily commitment eventually led to the honor of being one of the first-ever President Sea Scout Award Winners and receiving a Presidential Recognition Award. It taught me that real leadership isn't about standing at the front, but lifting the team from the center.

Chapter II

The Crucible: Naval Academy

Joining the Sri Lanka Navy as an Officer Under Trainee at the Naval and Maritime Academy was a reality check. I started with poor physical fitness, which meant facing daily, grueling challenges just to keep up. But through immense perseverance, I lost 40 kgs and forged a resilience I never knew I possessed.

The Academy was a profound teacher of true leadership—the kind that requires putting your people before yourself in the harshest conditions. Eventually, an unavoidable knee injury forced me to leave. I didn't see it as a defeat, but as a lesson in gracefully adapting to life's hardest pivots. The discipline of the Navy has never left me.

Chapter III

The Pivot: A Student's Mindset

Channeling the intense discipline forged in the Navy, I embraced a new beginning at SLIIT University, pursuing a BSc (Hons.) in Information Technology. I approached academia with the humility of a student eager to learn from scratch.

Currently in my final year, I am profoundly grateful for the opportunity to merge technical knowledge with the life lessons I learned in the uniform. Joining the SLIIT Gavel Club allowed me to face my oldest fear—public speaking—and eventually represent my university in national debate and speaking championships. Every speech was a step towards self-mastery.

Chapter IV

The Ascent: Fostering Teams

My entrance into the corporate world was built on a desire to support and elevate others. Starting from the ground up as a Business Partner Intern at MAS Holdings, I learned the intricate mechanics of organizational growth.

Taking on more responsibility, I served as an Executive in Global Branding and Talent Attraction, and later embraced creative challenges as the Head of Branding and Design Strategy at Strider Seven Group. Today, as a Business Partner Executive at Virtusa, my focus remains unchanged: fostering a positive culture, managing end-to-end HR operations, and genuinely supporting the 350+ individuals in my care. Progress, to me, is measured by the growth of the teams I work alongside.

Chapter V

The Voice: Empowering Others

A crucial part of my journey stems from a deep admiration for great speakers. Inspired by their ability to move people, I wanted to create a platform that gave others the same power. Alongside my university friends, we founded a start-up called Beyond the Mic.

What started as an idea grew into me becoming a Corporate Communication Trainer. I have since conducted over 50 training sessions across corporate, university, and school levels. My ultimate goal isn't just to speak well, but to strive to expand further, empowering nervous speakers to find their voice and helping people wherever I possibly can.
