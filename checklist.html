The 5-Minute
Executive Presence Checklist.

Command any room with confidence, clarity, and authority. Created by Eranga Bowatte.

1. Physical & Vocal Command

Anchor Your Posture

Keep your feet shoulder-width apart. Roll your shoulders back and down. A grounded physical presence instantly signals authority to your audience.

The "Pause" Over Filler Words

Whenever you feel an "um", "ah", or "like" coming, simply stop talking for 2 seconds. Silence portrays thoughtfulness; filler words portray nervousness.

2. Structural Clarity

Bottom-Line Up Front (BLUF)

Start your answers or presentations with the main conclusion or outcome first, then provide the supporting context. Executives value time over buildup.

Use the Rule of Three

Human brains process information best in threes. "We have three options..." or "There are three main challenges...". It frames you as highly organized.

3. Relational Engagement

Intentional Eye Contact

Hold eye contact with one person for an entire sentence or thought before moving to the next. Do not "scan" the room rapidly.
